Shooting Drills

1)Spot Shooting :
Mark several spots on the court (e.g., corners, wings, top of the key).

Shoot a certain number of shots from each spot, keeping track of makes and misses.

Move to the next spot after completing the set.

2) Running Shooting Drill:
Start at one end of the court and sprint to the opposite end.

Stop at a designated spot and shoot.

This drill improves your ability to shoot after running or changing pace.

3) Game-Like Shooting Drill :
Simulate game situations with a partner or in a team setting.

Use screens, dribble moves, and passes to create open shots.

This drill helps you apply your shooting skills in a more realistic context.

4) 3-Point Shooting Drill:
Set a timer and see how many 3-pointers you can make in a set time (e.g., 1 minute).

Alternatively, make a certain number of 3-pointers from each spot behind the arc before moving on to the next spot.

Passing

Pass and Cut Drill:
Players form a line at the top of the key and at one corner of the court.

The first player in the top line passes to the player in the corner, then cuts to the basket.

The corner player returns the pass for a layup or passes to another teammate if the cut is not open.

Two-Line Passing Drill:
Set up two lines of players facing each other with a coach or passer in the middle.

The coach passes to a player in one line, who then passes back before the coach quickly moves to pass to the opposite line.

Focus on chest passes and making the return pass quickly.

Shuffle Passing Drill:
Players form a line and pass the ball to the player on their left while shuffling to the right.

After a set number of passes, the direction is reversed.

This drill improves players' ability to pass while moving and maintaining defensive stance.

Defending

Coordinated movements:

Spread all the participating players out at even intervals, facing one side of the court in the defensive stance. From the sideline, the coach calls out which movements the players should perform, switching quickly through high-intensity exercises like close-outs, defensive slides, charges, and backpedals.

The drill typically involves a 3-on-3 or 4-on-4 setup, where the offense attempts to score against a set defense. The defenders must communicate and move as a unit, helping each other and rotating to cover any gaps that may appear. The emphasis is on maintaining proper defensive spacing, staying between the ball and the basket, and quickly adjusting to the offense's movements. By practicing this drill, teams can improve their defensive cohesion and become more adept at disrupting the opposing team's offensive flow. It's a valuable exercise for learning how to work together as a defense and for developing the instincts needed to anticipate and counteract offensive strategies




Dribbling Drills

Crossover

The crossover drill is another classic beginner dribbling drill. You’ll start with the same stance as the pound drill, but instead of dribbling with the same hand you’ll alternate hands and crossover repeatedly. The crossover is one of the most basic dribbling moves and this drill will help improve it.

Partner Drills

What to Do: Each partner has a ball. Start by dribbling in place, then move around each other, performing different dribbling moves. Variations: Increase speed, add a pass and catch component, or have one partner act as a defender trying to knock the ball away.

Plyometrics

Box Jumps

How to Do It: Stand in front of a sturdy box or platform. Bend your knees slightly, swing your arms, and explosively jump onto the box. Land softly, step or jump back down, and repeat. Why It's Effective: Box jumps improve explosive power and speed, essential for quick jumps during games. Perform the Jump: Bend your knees slightly, swing your arms back, and then explosively forward and up as you jump onto the box. Use your ankles, knees, and hips to generate maximum power. Aim to land on the center of the box with both feet at the same time. Perform the desired number of repetitions. Start with a lower number if you're a beginner and gradually increase as you build strength

Clap Pushups

What to Do: Clap Pushups are an advanced variation of pushups. It involves propelling your body off the ground with enough force to clap your hands in the air before catching yourself and performing another pushup. Frequency : 10-12 clap pushups a day will be impressive enough to build a body.
