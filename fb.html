Shooting Drills

1)Two Touch Shooting Drill :



Setup: Players on the goal line play a firm pass to players at the top of the box.

First Touch : Players receive the pass and with their first touch, set up a second touch shot.
The first touch should be diagonally ahead.

Shooting: The shot should be aimed at the far post.

Rotation: Players who pass follow their pass and go to the line at the top of the box,
while shooters go to the end of the "passers" line.

2)Dribble & Shoot Drill :

Setup: Set up cones about 10 meters from the goal. Players line up behind the cones.

Instructions: Players dribble as fast as possible towards the cone while keeping close control of the ball.
At the cone, they make a move (e.g., cut with the inside foot) and then cut in towards the net.
The final touch before the shot should be diagonal towards the goal. Players then take a shot on goal, aiming for the far post.

Coaching Points: Emphasize close control while dribbling at speed. Encourage players to lift their heads and pick a target before shooting.
Focus on accuracy over power. Ensure players do not stop after starting the dribble.

3)Jump and Shoot Drill :

Setup: Set up low hurdles or a speed ladder about 7 meters from the goal. Players line up behind the hurdles or speed ladder.

Instructions: Players hop over the hurdles or run through the speed ladder with quick, precise movements. As they complete the last hop or step, a server (another player or coach) plays a pass to them. The player receiving the pass should take a first touch to set up the shot and then shoot on goal. The shot should be taken quickly, ideally with the first touch after setting up.

Coaching Points: Focus on explosive movements while hopping or running through the ladder. Time the pass so that the player can smoothly transition from the hops to the shot. Encourage players to keep their eyes on the ball and make a quick, controlled shot. Start with shots from about 7 meters out and gradually increase the distance as players improve.

Passing

1)Pass and Follow Drill :



Setup: Set up cones in a straight line, about 5-10 meters apart. Players form lines behind each cone.

Instructions: The first player at cone 1 passes the ball to the first player at cone 2. After making the pass, the player from cone 1 follows their pass and moves to the back of the line at cone 2. The player at cone 2 receives the ball and passes it to the first player at cone 3, then follows their pass to the back of the line at cone 3. Continue this sequence down the line of cones.

Coaching Points: Emphasize accurate passes and good first touches. Encourage players to communicate and call for the ball. Focus on the timing of runs and passes to ensure smooth transitions.

2)One-Touch Passing Dril :

Setup: Set up a grid about 10x10 meters. Divide players into pairs, each pair with one ball.

Instructions: Players pass the ball back and forth using only one touch. Encourage players to keep the ball moving quickly and accurately. After a set time (e.g., 1 minute), have players switch partners and continue.

Coaching Points: Emphasize quick decision-making and accurate passing. Encourage players to stay light on their feet and be ready to receive the ball. Focus on communication and maintaining a good tempo.

3)Rondo Drill :

Setup: Create a small circle or square grid (about 5-7 meters in diameter). Place 4-6 players around the outside of the grid and 1-2 players inside the grid as defenders.

Instructions: The players on the outside pass the ball to each other, trying to maintain possession. The defenders inside the grid try to intercept the ball or force a mistake. If a defender wins the ball or forces it out of bounds, they switch places with the player who lost possession. The objective for the outside players is to complete as many passes as possible without losing the ball.

Coaching Points: Emphasize quick, accurate passes. Encourage players to use one or two touches to keep the ball moving. Players should communicate and call for the ball. Focus on good body positioning to receive the ball and make the next pass. Encourage players to keep their heads up and be aware of the defenders' positions.

Variations: Increase or decrease the number of defenders to adjust the difficulty. Limit the number of touches each player can take (e.g., one-touch passing). Change the size of the grid to make it easier or more challenging. Add a rule that requires a certain number of consecutive passes before a point is scored.

Defending

1. One-on-One Defending

Setup: Pair players into attackers and defenders. Set up a small grid (10x10 yards) with a goal at one end.

Execution: The attacker starts with the ball, and the defender's goal is to prevent the attacker from scoring. After a set time or a goal, players switch roles.

Focus: This drill emphasizes individual defending skills, including close marking, tackling, and interceptions.

2. Defensive Shape and Covering :

Setup: Organize a small-sided game (e.g., 4v4) in a larger grid (30x20 yards) with goals at each end.

Execution: Play a normal game, but the focus is on the defenders maintaining their shape and covering for each other.

Focus: This drill helps players understand the importance of defensive positioning and communication.

4. Sliding Tackle Practice

Setup: Create a straight line with cones about 20 yards long. Place a ball on one side of the cones.

Execution: Players line up on the other side. On command, they sprint towards the ball and perform a sliding tackle.

Focus: This drill helps players master the technique of sliding tackles, emphasizing safety and effectiveness.

Dribbling Drills

1. Cone Weave Drill

Setup: Place cones in a zigzag pattern about 2 yards apart. Start with a simple pattern and make it more complex as you improve.

Execution: Dribble the ball through the cones as quickly as possible, weaving in and out. Focus on keeping the ball close to your feet and changing direction smoothly.

Focus: This drill improves close control, agility, and the ability to change direction quickly.

2. The Gate Drill

Setup: Set up two small gates with cones. Players start at one end of the area.

Execution: On the coach's signal, players dribble towards the first gate, perform a move to get through, then dribble to the next gate.

Focus: This drill encourages players to use different moves and improves their ability to beat opponents in tight spaces.

GoalKeeper

1. Crosses and Corners :

Setup: Organize a small-sided game or a specific drill focusing on crosses and corners. The goalkeeper is the only defender in the box.

Execution: Players take turns delivering crosses or corners. The goalkeeper must communicate with imaginary defenders, position correctly, and make the necessary saves or catches.

Focus: This drill improves the goalkeeper's ability to deal with crosses and set pieces, including positioning, timing, and communication.

2. Penalty Saves :

Setup: The goalkeeper starts in the goal. A coach or teammate takes penalty kicks.

Execution: The goalkeeper must anticipate the direction of the shot and make the save.

Focus: This drill improves the goalkeeper's ability to read the shooter's intentions, as well as their reflexes and positioning.
